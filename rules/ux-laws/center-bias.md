## The Rule
- Place primary content at the center of the screen. Users fixate there first on the initial scan.
- Shift focal elements ~10% above the geometric midpoint to hit the optical center.
- Apply F-pattern for text-heavy pages, Z-pattern for visual pages, and center-first for landing pages.

## Why
- Eye-tracking shows first fixation lands at approximately 50-60% of page width and 40% of page height.
- Tatler (2007) found the central fixation bias persists regardless of image content and motor bias, pointing to the screen center as a convenient starting position for oculomotor exploration rather than a purely foveal advantage.
- The geometric center looks visually low. A slight upward offset is needed to appear balanced.

## When It Breaks
- Text-dense interfaces: F-pattern dominates and center-bias weakens.
- On mobile: center-bias is stronger due to the narrower visual field.

## In Practice
- Primary CTA: position vertically at 45-48% instead of 50%. It reads as better balanced.

## Key Numbers
- **~10%** — Optical center above geometric center
