## The Rule
- Clearspace is the minimum protected zone around a logo where no text, images, graphic elements, or competing marks may appear.
- Define the clearspace unit from a logo element. For wordmarks: typically the cap-height or x-height of the typeface. Cap-height for large-format use, x-height for smaller or text-adjacent contexts. For combination marks: typically the height of the symbol. Label the result 1x.
- Minimum clearspace: 1x on all four sides. Define it as a ratio, not a fixed pixel or millimeter value, so it scales with the logo.
- 1x is the mathematical minimum. Apply optical adjustment where geometry creates perceived imbalance: rounded shapes and diagonal elements may require additional space on specific sides.
- Design convention: no universal standard exists. Each brand defines its own unit. Document both the unit choice and the minimum explicitly.

## Why
- Visual proximity creates association. Elements inside the clearspace appear related to the logo, diluting brand distinction.
- Gestalt proximity principle: items close together are perceived as a group. Clearspace breaks that grouping around the mark.
- Without protected space, competing elements bleed into the logo at small sizes and reduce legibility.

## When It Breaks
- Lockup compositions: when a logo is intentionally paired with a tagline or co-brand mark, the combined unit defines its own outer clearspace boundary.
- Favicon and app icon: external clearspace rules do not apply because the platform container forms the boundary.
- Image backgrounds: clearspace cannot always be guaranteed on photos or complex visuals. Use a solid backing field (white, black, or brand color) behind the logo to create a guaranteed clear zone.

## In Practice
- Wordmark logo: use cap-height as 1x for large-format contexts, x-height for smaller and text-adjacent uses. Document the chosen unit explicitly: '1x clearspace required on all sides.'
- Combination mark: use the symbol height as 1x. Apply consistently across print, digital, and co-branding contexts.
- On image backgrounds: apply a solid backing field with 0.5x internal padding around the logo. Maintain standard 1x clearspace from the backing field edge outward.
- Before finalizing: test the defined clearspace unit in real layouts at minimum size, maximum size, and at least one edge-case context (co-branding, image background, constrained UI).
