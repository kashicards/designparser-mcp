## The Rule
- Use red for energy, urgency, and danger signals.
- Red triggers avoidance motivation and impairs performance in achievement contexts (Elliot et al., 2007, n=71, anagram and IQ tasks).
- Use blue for trust, calm, and competence. A strong Western convention, not a universal psychological constant.
- Use green for growth, success, and confirmation signals.
- Reserve green for positive action confirmations.
- Use yellow for optimism and warning contexts.
- Yellow has high luminance (Y ~0.93), causing poor contrast against light backgrounds. Both yellow and white are high-luminance, so their ratio collapses.
- Use black and white for premium and minimalist contexts.
- Choose black and white when emotional neutrality is required.
- All above associations reflect Western-market conventions. Cultural variation is significant. Validate for non-Western audiences.

## Why
- Blue is the dominant brand color in finance and tech, a widely observed industry pattern.
- This is a convention, not a measured psychological constant.
- Color triggers emotional responses via pre-attentive visual processing before conscious attention engages.
- Pre-attentive visual processing detects color in roughly 50-250ms. This is color recognition, not the emotional response, which emerges later and is harder to measure.
- Widely cited evolutionary hypothesis: red signals blood and danger, green signals safe vegetation. Not empirically confirmed as a general mechanism.
- Cultural layering applies: white means purity in the West.
- White signals mourning in parts of Asia.

## When It Breaks
- Cultural differences matter: green is sacred in Islamic contexts.
- Purple signals mourning in parts of Latin America.
- Color alone does not communicate meaning.
- Color blindness affects 8% of men.
- Purely color-coded signals are inaccessible to color blind users.
- Too many signal colors at once means none of them stand out.

## In Practice
- Red or orange for urgency.
- Blue for trust.
- Green for confirmation.
- Yellow for warnings.
- Never use all four signal colors on one page.
